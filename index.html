Budget Highlights

Total Receipts (excluding borrowings): ₹34.96 lakh crore

Total Expenditure: ₹50.65 lakh crore

Fiscal Deficit: 4.4% of GDP

Capital Expenditure: ₹11.21 lakh crore

Overall Goals & Theme

The Budget 2025-2026 aims to accelerate growth, secure inclusive development, and invigorate private sector investments to realize the vision of 'Viksit Bharat'.

  • Accelerate growth and secure inclusive development.
  • Invigorate private sector investments and enhance spending power.
  • Focus on 'Sabka Vikas' (Development for All) and realizing the vision of 'Viksit Bharat' (Developed India).
  • The budget focuses on ten broad areas centered around Garib (Poor), Youth, Annadata (Farmers), and Nari (Women).
  • The four key engines for development are Agriculture, MSMEs, Investment, and Exports, fueled by Reforms and guided by Inclusivity.
  • Major reforms are planned across six domains: Taxation, Power Sector, Urban Development, Mining, Financial Sector, and Regulatory Reforms.

Key Proposals (Part A) - Engines of Development

Agriculture (1st Engine)

Significant focus on boosting agricultural productivity and farmer prosperity.

  • Launch 'Prime Minister Dhan-Dhaanya Krishi Yojana' in 100 districts to boost productivity, diversification, storage, and credit access.
  • A multi-sectoral 'Rural Prosperity and Resilience' programme to address under-employment through skilling and investment.
  • A 6-year "Mission for Aatmanirbharta in Pulses" focusing on Tur, Urad, and Masoor, with assured procurement.
  • Comprehensive program for promoting production and processing of vegetables, fruits, and shree-anna.
  • Establishment of a Makhana Board in Bihar.
  • National Mission on High Yielding Seeds.
  • Framework for sustainable harnessing of fisheries.
  • 'Mission for Cotton Productivity' to improve yields and promote extra-long staple cotton.
  • Enhanced Kisan Credit Card (KCC) loan limit from ₹3 lakh to ₹5 lakh.
  • New Urea Plant in Namrup, Assam.
  • Repositioning India Post as a catalyst for the rural economy and a large public logistics organization.

MSMEs (2nd Engine)

Measures to support and grow Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises.

  • Enhanced investment and turnover limits for MSME classification (2.5x and 2x respectively).
  • Increased credit guarantee cover for Micro & Small Enterprises (to ₹10 crore), Startups (to ₹20 crore), and exporter MSMEs.
  • Customized Credit Cards with a ₹5 lakh limit for micro-enterprises.
  • New Fund of Funds for startups with a ₹10,000 crore contribution.
  • Scheme for 5 lakh women, SC, and ST first-time entrepreneurs offering loans up to ₹2 crore.
  • Focus Product Scheme for Footwear & Leather sectors.
  • Scheme to make India a global hub for toys.
  • National Institute of Food Technology, Entrepreneurship and Management in Bihar.
  • National Manufacturing Mission to further "Make in India", including Clean Tech manufacturing.

Investment (3rd Engine)

Boosting investment across people, economy, and innovation.

Investing in People:

  • Enhanced cost norms for Saksham Anganwadi and Poshan 2.0
  • 50,000 new Atal Tinkering Labs
  • Broadband to all government secondary schools and rural PHCs
  • Bharatiya Bhasha Pustak Scheme for digital Indian language books
  • Five National Centres of Excellence for skilling
  • Expansion of capacity in IITs
  • Centre of Excellence in AI for Education
  • 10,000 additional medical education seats
  • Day Care Cancer Centres in all district hospitals
  • Scheme for urban workers' upliftment
  • Revamped PM SVANidhi scheme for street vendors
  • Social security for online platform (gig) workers

Investing in the Economy:

  • 3-year PPP project pipeline for infrastructure
  • ₹1.5 lakh crore outlay for state capital expenditure loans
  • Second Asset Monetization Plan (2025-30) aiming for ₹10 lakh crore
  • Extension of Jal Jeevan Mission till 2028
  • Urban Challenge Fund of ₹1 lakh crore
  • Incentives for power sector reforms
  • Nuclear Energy Mission for Viksit Bharat aiming for 100 GW by 2047
  • Revamped Shipbuilding Financial Assistance Policy
  • Maritime Development Fund of ₹25,000 crore
  • Modified UDAN scheme for regional connectivity
  • Greenfield airports in Bihar
  • Support for Western Koshi Canal Project
  • Mining sector reforms
  • SWAMIH Fund 2 (₹15,000 crore) for stressed housing projects
  • Access to PM Gati Shakti data for the private sector
  • Development of top 50 tourist destinations

Investing in Innovation:

  • ₹20,000 crore allocation for private sector R&D initiative
  • Deep Tech Fund of Funds exploration
  • 10,000 PM Research Fellowships
  • 2nd Gene Bank for crop germplasm
  • National Geospatial Mission
  • Gyan Bharatam Mission for manuscript heritage

Exports (4th Engine)

Measures to promote Indian exports.

  • Export Promotion Mission with sectoral targets.
  • 'BharatTradeNet' (BTN) digital platform for international trade.
  • Support for domestic manufacturing integration with global supply chains.
  • National framework for promoting Global Capability Centres (GCCs) in tier 2 cities.
  • Upgradation of air cargo warehousing facilities.

Reforms (Fuel)

Key reforms across various sectors to facilitate growth and ease of doing business.

Tax Reforms:

  • Commitment to "trust first, scrutinize later"
  • Introduction of a new income-tax bill.

Financial Sector Reforms:

  • FDI limit in insurance raised to 100% (with conditions)
  • Expanded services of India Post Payment Bank
  • Credit Enhancement Facility by NaBFID
  • 'Grameen Credit Score' framework
  • Pension sector regulatory forum
  • Simplified KYC process
  • Rationalized company merger procedures
  • Revamped Bilateral Investment Treaties (BIT) model

Regulatory Reforms:

  • High-Level Committee for reviewing non-financial regulations
  • Investment Friendliness Index of States
  • FSDC mechanism to evaluate financial regulations
  • Jan Vishwas Bill 2.0 to decriminalize more provisions

Tax Proposals (Part B)

Indirect Taxes

Changes related to Customs duties and other indirect taxes.

  • Rationalization of Customs tariff structure, removing seven tariff rates.
  • Relief on import of 36 life-saving drugs (fully exempt from BCD) and 6 drugs (concessional 5% duty).
  • Full BCD exemption for more critical minerals like cobalt powder and waste/scrap of lithium-ion batteries, lead, zinc.
  • Support for domestic manufacturing in textiles (exempting certain looms), electronics (adjusting BCD on IFPD and components), and Li-ion batteries (exempting more capital goods).
  • BCD exemption on inputs for shipbuilding extended for 10 years.
  • Export promotion measures for handicrafts, leather (exempting Wet Blue BCD, removing export duty on crust leather), and marine products.
  • Trade facilitation measures like a time limit for provisional assessments (2 years, extendable by 1) and voluntary compliance declarations.

Direct Taxes

Changes related to Income Tax and other direct taxes.

New Income Tax Regime Reforms (Focus on Middle Class):

Significant changes to tax slabs and rates in the new regime.

  • No income tax payable up to an income of ₹12 lakh (₹12.75 lakh for salaried individuals due to standard deduction).
  • Revised tax slabs and rates:
  • Tax rebate provided for incomes up to ₹12 lakh to ensure zero tax liability.

TDS/TCS Rationalization:

  • Reduced number of rates
  • Increased thresholds (e.g., ₹1 lakh for senior citizen interest TDS, ₹6 lakh for rent TDS, ₹10 lakh for LRS TCS )
  • Removal of TCS on education remittances via loans
  • Omission of TCS on sale of goods
  • Higher TDS only in non-PAN cases.

Voluntary Compliance:

  • Time limit to file updated returns extended from 2 years to 4 years.

Reduced Compliance Burden:

  • Registration period for small charitable trusts increased to 10 years
  • Benefit of two self-occupied properties allowed without conditions
  • Omission of TCS on sale of specified goods
  • Removal of higher TDS/TCS for non-filers.

Ease of Doing Business:

  • Scheme for determining arm's length price for 3 years
  • Expanded scope of safe harbour rules
  • Exemption for withdrawals from old NSS accounts.

Employment & Investment:

  • Presumptive tax regime for non-residents providing services for electronics manufacturing
  • Extension of Tonnage Tax Scheme to inland vessels
  • 5-year extension for startup incorporation benefits
  • Benefits for IFSC units (ship-leasing, insurance, treasury centres) extended
  • Extension of investment date for Sovereign/Pension Funds.

Fiscal Policy

Details on the government's financial targets and estimates.

  • Commitment to fiscal consolidation, aiming for a declining central government debt-to-GDP ratio.
  • Revised fiscal deficit estimate for 2024-25: 4.8% of GDP.
  • Budgeted fiscal deficit for 2025-26: 4.4% of GDP.
  • Total receipts (excluding borrowings) estimated at ₹34.96 lakh crore for 2025-26.
  • Total expenditure estimated at ₹50.65 lakh crore for 2025-26.
  • Net market borrowings estimated at ₹11.54 lakh crore for 2025-26.
