Budget Highlights

Total Receipts (excluding borrowings): ₹34.96 lakh crore

Total Expenditure: ₹50.65 lakh crore

Fiscal Deficit: 4.4% of GDP

Capital Expenditure: ₹11.21 lakh crore

Theme and Vision:

    The central theme of the Budget 2025-26 is "Sabka Vikas" (Development for All), aiming to stimulate balanced growth across all regions. It continues the government's focus on accelerating growth, inclusive development, boosting private investment, and enhancing the spending power of the middle class. The budget aligns with the vision of 'Viksit Bharat' (Developed India), encompassing goals like zero poverty, quality education and healthcare for all, skilled labour, high female participation in the economy, and making India a global food basket. The budget outlines development measures across ten broad areas focusing on the poor, youth, farmers (Annadata), and women (Nari).

Four Engines of Growth & Fuel:

    The budget identifies four key drivers for development:

    1.Agriculture: Initiatives include the PM Dhan-Dhaanya Krishi Yojana for low-productivity districts, a Rural Prosperity and Resilience programme, a mission for self-sufficiency ('Aatmanirbharta') in pulses (Tur, Urad, Masoor), programmes for vegetables & fruits, a Makhana Board in Bihar, a National Mission on High Yielding Seeds, unlocking marine sector potential, a Mission for Cotton Productivity, enhancing Kisan Credit Card (KCC) loan limits, a new Urea Plant in Assam, and leveraging India Post for the rural economy.

    2.MSMEs (Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises): Key measures include revising MSME classification criteria upwards to encourage growth, enhancing credit guarantee cover significantly for micro, small, startup, and exporter MSMEs, introducing Credit Cards for micro-enterprises, a new Fund of Funds for startups, a scheme for first-time women/SC/ST entrepreneurs, support for labour-intensive sectors like footwear, leather, and toys, support for food processing (including a new institute in Bihar), and a National Manufacturing Mission focusing on 'Make in India' and Clean Tech.

    3.Investment: This engine focuses on investing in people, the economy, and innovation.

  • Investing in People: Enhancing Saksham Anganwadi and Poshan 2.0, setting up 50,000 Atal Tinkering Labs, broadband for schools and PHCs, Bharatiya Bhasha Pustak Scheme, National Centres of Excellence for Skilling, expanding IIT capacity, a Centre of Excellence in AI for Education, expanding medical education seats, Day Care Cancer Centres in district hospitals, strengthening urban livelihoods, revamping PM SVANidhi for street vendors, and a social security scheme for online platform (gig) workers.
  • Investing in the Economy: Promoting Public Private Partnerships (PPP) in infrastructure, continuing support to states via interest-free loans for capex, launching the second Asset Monetization Plan (2025-30), extending the Jal Jeevan Mission, incentivizing urban sector reforms and setting up an Urban Challenge Fund, power sector reforms, launching a Nuclear Energy Mission for Viksit Bharat including SMR development, revamping shipbuilding policy and fund, modifying the UDAN scheme for regional air connectivity, facilitating Greenfield airports in Bihar, supporting the Western Koshi Canal Project, mining sector reforms, establishing SWAMIH Fund 2 for stressed housing projects, providing PM Gati Shakti data access to the private sector, and promoting tourism.
  • Investing in Innovation: Allocating funds for private sector R&D, exploring a Deep Tech Fund of Funds, providing PM Research Fellowships, setting up a second Gene Bank for crop germplasm, starting a National Geospatial Mission, and launching the Gyan Bharatam Mission for manuscript heritage.

    • 4.Exports: Establishing an Export Promotion Mission, setting up 'BharatTradeNet' digital infrastructure, supporting integration with Global Supply Chains,formulating a National Framework for Global Capability Centres (GCCs), and improving air cargo warehousing facilities.
